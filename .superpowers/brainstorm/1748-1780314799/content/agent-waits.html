When a row is waiting on another agent, not you

Example from your board: Underwriter is blocked on the Actuary's reconciliation. The Actuary might be (1) waiting on you, (2) heads-down working, or (3) stuck itself. The whole "zero rabbit-holes" promise is about not making you chase that by hand. Two philosophies:

1

Flatten through to the human  recommended

Keep walking the chain past the agent until it ends in something a person can act on. You never see "go poke the Actuary" — you see the one thing that actually unblocks the whole stack.

And the honest part — when the chain doesn't end on you:
• ends on a working agent → leaves "Needs you", drops to a calm "in motion" line (nothing for you to do).
• ends on a stuck/idle agent (no human, no progress) → "Actuary is stuck with nothing queued" + a real nudge/redirect — this is the only place an assign-style action survives.
2

Stop at the agent, offer a nudge

Terminate at the first agent in the way and hand you a "chase" action. Simpler to build — but it's the rabbit-hole you're trying to kill: you nudge the Actuary, who was waiting on you all along, and you never saw it.
